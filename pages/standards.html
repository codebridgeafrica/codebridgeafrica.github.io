CSTA K–12 Computer Science Standards

CSTA

The Computer Science Teachers Association K–12 standards define what students at each grade band should know and be able to do. Our Kids and Teen programmes are directly mapped to these five strands:

Algorithms & Programming — decomposition, variables, loops, functions
Data & Analysis — collection, storage, visualisation, inference
Computing Systems — hardware, software, troubleshooting
Networks & the Internet — protocols, cybersecurity, infrastructure
Impacts of Computing — ethics, privacy, digital citizenship
csteachers.org/page/standards

ACM Computing Curricula

ACM

The Association for Computing Machinery publishes comprehensive curriculum guidelines for CS education at secondary and post-secondary levels. Our adult and university programmes adopt these key components:

Computational Thinking — abstraction, pattern recognition, algorithm design
Software Engineering — version control, testing, documentation
Professional Practice — communication, ethics, teamwork
Specialised Areas — web, databases, AI/ML, systems, networking
acm.org/education/curricula-recommendations

UNESCO ICT Competency Framework for Teachers

UNESCO

The UNESCO ICT-CFT guides how technology is integrated into education. Our instructor training aligns with its three progressive stages:

1
Technology Literacy — Instructors understand and use digital tools to support student learning effectively
2
Knowledge Deepening — Instructors use technology to support problem-based, collaborative learning
3
Knowledge Creation — Instructors help students generate new knowledge and innovate using technology
unesco.org — ICT Competency Framework
