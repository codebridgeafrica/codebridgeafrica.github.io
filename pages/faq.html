Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before getting started. Can't find your answer? Contact us directly.

Admissions & Enrolment
Anyone! We cater for ages 6 and above across five pathways: CodeBridge Kids (6–12), Teen Techies (13–19), University Students, Adults & Professionals (18+), and Career Switchers. No prior coding experience is required for any beginner course — we start from absolute zero.
Fill in the registration form on our homepage — it takes about 3 minutes. Our admissions team will be in touch to confirm your place and provide payment details. You can also WhatsApp or call us directly on +233 245 676 676.
For beginner courses, no test is required. For intermediate and advanced courses, we may do a brief skills placement check — not to gate-keep, but to make sure you're placed in the right level so you get the most out of the programme.
We cap classes at 12–15 students depending on the programme. Small classes mean every student gets direct attention, can ask questions freely, and gets hands-on support during projects.
Fees & Payment
Fees depend on the programme: Kids courses start from GHS 350/month, Teen Techies from GHS 399/month, and Adult/University tracks from GHS 699/month. All fees are published transparently on each pathway page — no hidden charges.
Yes. You can pay monthly, termly (every 3 months), or in full upfront. We also periodically offer merit-based scholarships and subsidised places. Follow our social media pages to be notified when these open.
MTN Mobile Money, Vodafone Cash, AirtelTigo Money, bank transfer, and cash at our centre. Debit/credit card payments are coming soon. Always request an official receipt for every payment made.
Schedules & Delivery
We offer three modes: In-Person (Ashaley Botwe, Accra), Online (Zoom/Google Meet), and Hybrid. Most courses support all three. Check individual pathway pages for the modes available in the upcoming cohort.
Each course is 6 months, structured into 3 phases of 8 weeks each. Phases build on each other: Foundation → Build → Project/Launch. This structure gives enough time for real learning, not just surface knowledge.
A laptop or desktop computer is strongly recommended for coding courses (not just a phone). For Kids beginners, a tablet with keyboard may work for the first phase, but a laptop is needed from Phase 2 onward. We send a full setup guide before the first class.
Certificates & Recognition
Our certificates are recognised by our growing network of partner employers across Ghana and West Africa. We are working toward national TVET accreditation as part of our institutional roadmap. Beyond the certificate, our graduates carry strong portfolio projects — employers care about what you can build, not just the paper.
Both. A digitally signed PDF certificate you can share on LinkedIn, and a physical certificate awarded at our termly graduation ceremonies. Each certificate includes a unique verification code for employer use.
IT Services
From solo freelancers needing a website to large organisations needing network infrastructure. Our focus is SMEs across Ghana who want reliable, affordable IT without hiring a full-time IT team. We also work with schools, NGOs, and government agencies.
Brochure websites: 2–4 weeks. E-commerce stores: 4–8 weeks. Custom web applications: 8–24 weeks depending on complexity. We provide a detailed timeline in our written proposal — and we stick to it. We'd rather be honest about timelines than overpromise.
Yes. We offer flexible monthly maintenance and support retainers after project delivery. This covers updates, bug fixes, security patches, and minor changes. Ask about our post-launch support plans during the proposal phase.
